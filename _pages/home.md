Welcome! I am a computer scientist who grapples with **big data** at the intersection of 
**Artificial Intelligence** and scientific discovery. My research develops machine learning 
methods for high-stakes rare-event prediction — with a primary focus on **AI-driven space 
weather forecasting** — and builds open-source tools that make AI accessible to domain 
scientists across disciplines.

I am a **Distinguished University Professor** and **2CI Professor of Computer Science** at 
**Georgia State University**, where I founded and direct the 
[Data Mining Lab (DMLab)](https://dmlab.cs.gsu.edu/). I also hold affiliate appointments 
in the **Department of Physics & Astronomy** and the **Robinson College of Business** 
(Institute for Insight).

## Research Highlights

- **AI for Space Weather** — Deep learning models for solar flare, CME, and SEP prediction
- **Spatiotemporal Data Mining** — Methods for imbalanced, rare-event scientific datasets  
- **AI Surrogate Modeling** — Neural operators accelerating heliospheric MHD simulation
- **Open Science** — SWAN-SF dataset and MVTS-Data Toolkit used by the global community
